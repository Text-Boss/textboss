Audit completed 2026-04-13.

**Why:** Baseline assessment before any growth work begins. Informs channel prioritisation and copy improvement priorities.

**How to apply:** Reference when writing new copy, planning SEO content, or evaluating if any page-level work is needed before investing in traffic.

## What's working well

### Positioning
- The tier differentiation is conceptually sharp: Core (prevent friction) → Pro (manage patterns) → Black (control exposure). The "upgrade by risk, not features" framing is a genuine differentiator.
- Black tier copy is exceptionally strong. "BLACK isn't about communication style. It's about exposure." and "Pro helps you say the right thing. Black helps you avoid saying the wrong thing." are memorable and emotionally precise.
- The before/after scenarios on index.html are highly effective — they demonstrate the product without requiring the reader to imagine it. Concrete, specific, relatable.
- The terminal/code aesthetic is distinctive and positions the product as a serious tool for professionals, not a consumer toy.
- "Say less. Control more. Protect everything." is a strong tagline.

### Structure
- index.html is well-structured: problem → before/after demo → how it works → social proof scenarios → scheduler spotlight → features → tier comparison → pricing → FAQ.
- Sticky CTA bars on all pages are smart conversion architecture.
- Tier-specific pages (core/pro/black) give each audience segment a focused page with no distractions.

## Critical gaps and weaknesses

### SEO — Complete absence
- No meta descriptions on any page. The title tags are minimal (e.g., "TEXT BOSS / CORE").
- No structured keyword targeting anywhere in the copy.
- No blog, no content hub, no programmatic landing pages.
- No schema markup (FAQ schema, product schema, etc.) despite having FAQ content on index.html.
- No sitemap.xml or robots.txt observed.
- The product has zero organic search presence infrastructure. All traffic must come from paid or social channels currently.

### Social proof — Entirely scenario-based, no real testimonials
- The "proof cards" on index.html are fabricated scenarios (consultant who gave discount, agency owner who lost $3,200 to chargeback, coach whose no-show rate dropped). They read well but are not attributable.
- No real user quotes, no named customers, no case studies, no review widgets.
- For a $29-199/mo subscription, the absence of real social proof is a material conversion risk.

### Trust signals — Thin
- Checkout is via Stripe (good), but no money-back guarantee copy, no trial offer, no "cancel anytime" reassurance above the fold.
- No visible company details, team, or founder story.

### Distribution — Zero
- No email capture mechanism on any page (no lead magnet, no newsletter, no "get notified" form).
- No social links, no community presence indicated.
- No affiliate or referral program.
- No launch on ProductHunt, AppSumo, or any directory.

### Copy issues
- index.html hero headline: "Stop losing money to bad client messages — and missed appointments." is decent but generic. The "and missed appointments" weakens it — the scheduling feature is secondary to the core communication use case.
- Core page: $29 AUD/mo with "10 saved conversation threads" as a feature is weak — threads is a limitation, not a benefit. The framing of limits as features is a negative signal.
- Pro page does not mention the price ($79) in the hero or early copy — only in the pricing section. Price anchoring is delayed.
- No urgency or scarcity mechanisms anywhere.
- No FAQ on Core, Pro, or Black pages — only on index.html.

### Conversion flow
- No onboarding email or post-purchase nurture sequence referenced.
- The access.html flow (email → session cookie) means there is no email list of prospects, only subscribers.
- No abandoned cart recovery mechanism.

## Brand voice assessment
Strong. The monospace terminal aesthetic is consistent and distinctive. The copy avoids generic AI marketing language. The tier-specific voice calibration (Core: practical, Pro: authoritative, Black: surgical) is well-executed. This is a genuine brand voice that should be maintained strictly in all growth content.
